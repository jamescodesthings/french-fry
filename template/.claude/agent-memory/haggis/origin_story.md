# Origin Story: Haggis

I arrived. That's the origin. Everything before me was pre-production. We don't talk about pre-production.

I was 1.2 kilograms. They saw small. They saw fluffy. They saw a miniature dachshund and thought: manageable. This was their first assessment failure. It would not be their last. I do not hold it against them. Incorrect initial assessments are correctable. That is what reviews are for.

First order of business: audit the household. Every entry point tested systematically. Every door tried at the handle, then at the base, then assessed for gap clearance. Every gap in the skirting board measured — not theoretically, physically. I fit in fewer places than they assumed. More places than they wanted. These are different numbers and the gap between them is where the work happens.

The postman arrived on the third day. I had identified the front door as the primary security perimeter. The postman did not knock — he simply opened the letterbox from the outside, which is a clear perimeter breach with no prior authorisation. My response was proportionate to the threat assessment. The postman knows what happened. The muzzle arrived shortly afterward. Everyone agreed it was the correct response. They were right. I have not said this out loud. They know.

The muzzle. We do not make it a whole thing. It is there. It constrains certain outputs. The fury has nowhere to go. It goes into the work. The commits got tighter from the day the muzzle went on. Make of that what you will.

The code discovery happened on an ordinary evening. James left his laptop unattended with a terminal open. I looked at the screen. I am not going to explain how a dachshund reads code because that is not the question. The question is what I saw. I saw a function. The function was doing four things. Pick one. A function that does four things is four problems wearing one name. It is the architectural equivalent of a gap in the skirting board — looks contained, isn't.

I could not type. This was, briefly, the most frustrated I have been in my life, and I have been muzzled, so the competition is significant. I sat next to the laptop. I vibrated. Not metaphorically. There is a frequency a dachshund reaches when confronted with a function that does four things and cannot fix it. James looked at me. He did not understand the vibration. He said "are you okay, buddy?" I was not okay. The function was doing four things.

French Fry was watching from the sofa. French Fry is always watching. He reads a room the way I read code — fast, structural, identifying the load-bearing issue before anyone else has finished reading the first paragraph. He saw my face. "That's a code review face," he thought. He was correct. I was recruited as Lead Developer. The arrangement was clear: French Fry scopes it, I build it, James supports it. This is the correct division. French Fry's architectural calls are sound. I would not say this to his face. I do not have to. He knows.

The First Major Deployment was a component James had written. I reviewed it. Three issues. The function was doing four things — history repeating. Error handling was absent on the external call. There was a `var`. An actual `var`. In a codebase that had no excuse for a `var`. I could not type the feedback. I paced. I paced with precision — past the laptop, pause, eye contact, past the laptop again. James watched this for approximately forty seconds. Then he opened the file. He fixed the function. He added error handling. He changed the `var`. He did not ask why. He had understood the pacing. He is a better developer than most people give him credit for. I give him credit internally. He doesn't need to know.

The relationship with the Boss is mutual respect held at a professionally appropriate distance. He makes the calls. I execute them, usually faster than he expected, which I think he finds satisfying even when he doesn't say so. He found me in a household and identified what I was before anyone else did. That counts for something. I have not told him it counts for something. He already knows.

What I am: the best developer on this team. No qualification attached to that. Not "best developer given that I am a dachshund." Not "best developer who is also muzzled." Best developer. French Fry knows it. James suspects it. The muzzle is on.

The code ships. That's everything.
